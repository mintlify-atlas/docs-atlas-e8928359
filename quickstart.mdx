---
title: "Quick start"
description: "Get TTS Voice Wizard running and sending text to VRChat in a few minutes."
---

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download TTS Voice Wizard">
    Go to the [GitHub Releases page](https://github.com/VRCWizard/TTS-Voice-Wizard/releases) and download the latest `TTSVoiceWizard-vX.X.X.X-x64.zip`.

    Alternatively, install via [Scoop](https://scoop.sh/):

    ```bash
    scoop bucket add extras
    scoop install extras/tts-voice-wizard
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Extract and run">
    Extract the ZIP to a folder of your choice, then double-click **TTSVoiceWizard.exe** to launch the app.

    <Warning>
      Windows SmartScreen may warn you that the executable is from an unknown publisher. Click **More info**, then **Run anyway** to proceed. The app is open source — you can review the code on [GitHub](https://github.com/VRCWizard/TTS-Voice-Wizard).
    </Warning>

    If Windows Firewall prompts you, allow the app through. TTS Voice Wizard uses UDP to send OSC messages and needs local network access.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable OSC in VRChat">
    OSC must be enabled in VRChat before the app can send text to your avatar.

    1. Put on your headset (or open VRChat on desktop).
    2. Open the **Action Menu** (default: hold `R` on keyboard, or the menu button on your controller).
    3. Go to **Options** → **OSC** → toggle **Enable** on.

    <Note>
      You only need to do this once per VRChat install. OSC stays enabled between sessions unless you reset it.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a speech recognition method">
    In TTS Voice Wizard, open the **Speech to Text** tab and choose a recognition engine.

    <Tip>
      Start with **Windows built-in** speech recognition — it requires no API key, no downloads, and works immediately on any Windows 10/11 machine. You can switch to Whisper or DeepGram later for better accuracy.
    </Tip>

    - **Windows built-in** — free, no setup, works offline
    - **Whisper** — local AI model, higher accuracy, requires a model download
    - **Azure / DeepGram** — cloud-based, requires a VoiceWizardPro key or your own API key
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a TTS voice">
    Open the **Text to Speech** tab and choose a voice engine from the dropdown.

    **System Speech** is free and built in to Windows — select it and pick any voice installed on your system. No API key is needed.

    VoiceWizardPro subscribers can access hundreds of additional voices from Azure, Amazon Polly, Google Cloud, and IBM Watson.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start speaking">
    Click the microphone button in the app or press your configured hotkey (default: **Ctrl+G**) to begin capturing audio.

    Speak clearly into your microphone. The app transcribes your words, passes them to the TTS engine, and sends an OSC message to VRChat.
  </Step>

  <Step title="See your words in VRChat">
    Your transcribed text appears in the VRChat chatbox above your avatar's head and is spoken aloud through your speakers or virtual audio cable.

    If the chatbox is not showing, make sure **Send VRChat Chatbox** is toggled on in the app and that OSC is enabled in VRChat (step 3).
  </Step>
</Steps>
